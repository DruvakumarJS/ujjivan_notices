Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has issued instructions to banks on the facilities offered to customers who are old / sick / incapacitated and are not willing to open or operate joint accounts. Types of old / sick / incapacitated customers Customers who are old / sick / incapacitated fall under two categories: a. A customer who is too old or ill to sign a cheque or cannot be physically present in the bank to withdraw money from his or her account, but can put his or her thumb impression on the cheque or withdrawal form. b. A customer who is unable to be physically present in the bank and is also unable to put his or her thumb impression on the cheque or withdrawal form due to certain physical incapacities. Operational Procedure With a view to enabling old / sick / incapacitated customers to operate their bank accounts, RBI has directed banks to follow the procedure as under: (a) Wherever thumb or toe impression of the old / sick / incapacitated customer is obtained, he or she should be identified by two independent witnesses known to the bank, one of whom should be a responsible bank official. (b) Where the customer cannot even he put his or her thumb or toe impression, and would also not be able to be physically present in the bank, a mark can be obtained on the cheque or withdrawal form and he or she should be identified by two independent witnesses known to the bank, one of whom should be a responsible bank official. (c) The customer may also be asked to indicate to the bank as to who would withdraw the amount from the bank on the basis of the cheque or withdrawal form as obtained above and that person should be identified by two independent witnesses. The person who would actually be drawing money from the bank should be asked to furnish his or her full signature to the bank. If a customer cannot sign a cheque or withdrawal form due to loss of both hands, the signature can be made by means of a mark. The mark can be placed by the customer in any manner, including toe impression as suggested.
