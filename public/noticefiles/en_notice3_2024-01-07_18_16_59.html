Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has issued instructions to banks on the facilities offered to customers who are old / sick / incapacitated and are not willing to open or operate joint accounts.

A customer who is too old or ill to sign a cheque or cannot be physically present in the bank to withdraw money from his or her account, but can put his or her thumb impression on the cheque or withdrawal form.

A customer who is unable to be physically present in the bank and is also unable to put his or her thumb impression on the cheque or withdrawal form due to certain physical incapacities.

With a view to enabling old / sick / incapacitated customers to operate their bank accounts, RBI has directed banks to follow the procedure as under:

If a customer cannot sign a cheque or withdrawal form due to loss of both hands, the signature can be made by means of a mark. The mark can be placed by the customer in any manner, including toe impression as suggested. It can be by means of mark which anybody can put on behalf of the person who has to sign, the mark being put by an instrument which has had a physical contact with the person who has to sign, as held by the Supreme Court of India in AIR 1950 Supreme Court 265
